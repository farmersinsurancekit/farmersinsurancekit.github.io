Free Resources to Help You Study for the Insurance Exam

  • Home
  • Blog
  • Blog Details

Preparing for the insurance exam can be a daunting task, especially when you’re juggling work, family, and other responsibilities. Fortunately, there are plenty of free resources available to help you study effectively without breaking the bank. Whether you're aiming for a life and health insurance license or property and casualty certification, these tools can give you the edge you need to pass on your first attempt.

Why Free Study Resources Matter

In today’s economy, where inflation and rising costs are squeezing budgets, free study materials are more valuable than ever. Many aspiring insurance professionals are already balancing student loans, living expenses, and career transitions. Paying hundreds of dollars for exam prep courses isn’t always feasible. That’s why leveraging free resources can be a game-changer—helping you save money while still getting high-quality preparation.

The Impact of AI and Digital Learning

With advancements in AI and digital education, free resources have become more interactive and personalized. Many platforms now use adaptive learning technology to tailor study plans based on your strengths and weaknesses. This means you can focus on the areas where you need the most improvement, making your study time more efficient.

Top Free Resources for Insurance Exam Prep

1. State Insurance Department Websites

Every state has its own insurance department website, which often provides free study guides, exam outlines, and even sample questions. These materials are tailored to your state’s specific licensing requirements, ensuring you’re studying the right content.

How to Use Them:

  • Download the official exam content outline.
  • Review state-specific laws and regulations.
  • Take advantage of any free practice tests offered.

2. YouTube Channels and Video Lectures

YouTube is a goldmine for free insurance exam prep content. Channels like Insurance Exam Queen and ExamFX offer bite-sized lessons on key topics, from policy provisions to state regulations.

Recommended Channels:

  • Insurance Exam Queen – Great for breaking down complex concepts.
  • ExamFX Free Resources – Offers mini-lessons on high-yield topics.
  • Kaplan Financial Education – Provides free webinars and study tips.

3. Mobile Apps for On-the-Go Learning

If you’re always on the move, mobile apps can help you squeeze in study sessions during commutes or breaks. Many apps offer flashcards, quizzes, and progress tracking—all for free.

Best Free Apps:

  • Quizlet – Search for insurance exam flashcard sets created by other users.
  • Brainscape – Uses spaced repetition to reinforce key concepts.
  • Insurance Exam Prep (by Mometrix) – Offers free practice questions.

4. Online Forums and Study Groups

Connecting with others who are also preparing for the exam can provide motivation and additional insights. Websites like Reddit (r/Insurance) and Facebook study groups allow you to ask questions, share resources, and get advice from those who’ve already passed.

Tips for Engaging in Forums:

  • Ask specific questions (e.g., “How do you remember different types of policies?”).
  • Share free resources you’ve found helpful.
  • Participate in study challenges or accountability groups.

5. Free Practice Tests and Quizzes

Taking practice tests is one of the most effective ways to prepare. Many websites offer free sample questions that mimic the actual exam format.

Where to Find Them:

  • NAIC (National Association of Insurance Commissioners) – Provides sample questions for some state exams.
  • ExamFX Free Practice Test – Limited free questions, but useful for baseline assessment.
  • State-specific DOI websites – Some states publish past exam questions.

6. Public Libraries and Open Educational Resources (OER)

Don’t overlook your local library—many offer free access to insurance exam prep books through platforms like OverDrive or Libby. Additionally, OER platforms like OpenStax and MERLOT sometimes have relevant materials.

How to Maximize Library Resources:

  • Check out physical or digital copies of exam prep books.
  • Look for audiobooks if you prefer listening over reading.
  • Ask librarians for recommendations—they might know hidden gems.

How to Create a Study Plan Using Free Resources

Step 1: Assess Your Weak Areas

Take a diagnostic practice test to identify which topics need the most attention.

Step 2: Mix Different Formats

Combine videos, flashcards, and quizzes to keep your study sessions engaging.

Step 3: Set a Realistic Schedule

Even 30 minutes a day can make a difference if you stay consistent.

Step 4: Track Your Progress

Use apps or spreadsheets to monitor improvement in practice test scores.

Final Thoughts

With the right strategy, you can pass the insurance exam without spending a fortune. By leveraging free resources—from state-provided materials to YouTube tutorials and mobile apps—you’ll be well-prepared to ace the test and launch your career in insurance.
